How teachers can help

Sedentary habits among students, especially between the ages of 14 and 18, are increasingly common due to prolonged hours of studying, screen time, and minimal physical activity. This lifestyle can lead to physical issues such as obesity, muscle stiffness, poor posture, and long-term cardiovascular problems. Teachers play a vital role in addressing this issue as they directly influence students’ daily routines and habits.

Teachers can encourage physical movement by integrating brief activities into their classes. Simple adjustments like asking students to stand up and stretch between lessons, incorporating movement-based learning, or encouraging walking discussions for group work can reduce prolonged sitting. Additionally, promoting active commuting (walking or cycling to school) and educating students about the dangers of inactivity during health or homeroom periods can raise awareness.

Collaboration with parents can further enhance efforts to reduce sedentary habits. Teachers can communicate the importance of physical activity at parent-teacher meetings and suggest ways for parents to encourage exercise at home. Schools can also support teachers by organizing workshops and providing resources that help incorporate physical activity into classroom settings without compromising academic goals.

Benefits of and how to incorporate exercises

Long periods of continuous study can lead to fatigue, decreased concentration, and diminished academic performance. Study breaks, especially those involving physical activity, have been shown to improve attention spans, memory retention, and overall mental well-being. For students aged 14 to 18, incorporating short breaks during or between classes can mitigate the negative effects of prolonged sitting.

Teachers can implement brief stretching exercises, breathing exercises, or quick movement activities lasting 3-5 minutes during class transitions. Activities like shoulder rolls, leg stretches, or standing exercises can be performed at students’ desks without requiring significant time or space. Incorporating these breaks can refresh students’ focus and make learning sessions more effective.

In addition to physical exercises, mental breaks—such as mindful breathing or brief periods of silence—can also help reset students' focus. Schools can support teachers by scheduling longer breaks between classes or dedicating specific times in the day for physical activity. Using technology, teachers can show guided exercise videos or play quick games that involve movement to keep students engaged.
